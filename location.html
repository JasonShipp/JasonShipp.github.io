Richmond houses a wonderful array of attractions, either on its doorstep or within neighbouring towns, each highly accessible from our apartments.

Nature

Richmond is one of London's leafiest Boroughs, home to the deer filled Richmond Park and a beautiful stretch of the Thames Riverside, providing walkin routes aplenty. In neighbouring Kew, there is the Royal Botanic Gardens, home to some of the most exotic plants in the world, the worlds largest glass house and plenty more attractions, including music festivals, light shows and acrobatic displays.

Culture

Our apartments are situated only a few minutes' walk from two live theatres: Richmond Theatre and The Orange Tree Theatre. The latter provides a unique, up-close experience in an intimate venue (to secure tickets it is best to book in advance). A boat, bus or train away is the spectacular Hampton Court Palace, once home to King Henry VIII, containing beautiful grounds and a maze for children to enjoy.

Sport

Local to Richmond are two of the most iconic sporting venues London has to offer: Twickenham Rugby Ground and the All England Tennis Club, Wimbledon. Twickenham is the home of English Rugby Union, and hosts both international and domestic matches, along with music events. Wimbledon represents one of the four majors of the tennis circuit. If you are lucky enough to get tickets, both are thoroughly worth the money. On a smaller scale, there is an indoor and outdoor swimming pool, to cool off during those hotter days.

Shopping & Socialising

Richmond town centre is bustling with life, housing a wonderful array of pubs, bars, restaurants and cafes to enjoy, along with a cinema and a number of boutique shops. If that isn't enough, why not take a direct train to Europe's largest indoor shopping centre- Westfield Stratford, with an abundance of high-street and designer brands to indulge in.
